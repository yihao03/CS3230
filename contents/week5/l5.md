## Union bound

<div id="union-bound">

- You want to upper bound the probability that a bad event $\varepsilon$ occurs.
- You know that $\varepsilon = \varepsilon_1 ∨ \varepsilon_2 ∨ ⋯ ∨ \varepsilon_n$.
- Union bound:
  - $Pr[\varepsilon]= Pr[\varepsilon_1]∨[\varepsilon_2] ∨ ⋯ ∨[\varepsilon_n] ≤ Pr[\varepsilon1]+ Pr[\varepsilon_2]+ ⋯ + Pr[\varepsilon_n]$ .
- To make sure that $Pr[\varepsilon] \le f$, it suffices that $Pr[\varepsilon_i] \le \frac{f}{n}$ for each $i \in [n]$ .

</div>

## Markov's inequality

<div id="markov-inequality">

if $X$ is a non-negative random variable and $a > 0$, then

$$
Pr[X \ge a \cdot E[X]] \le \frac{1}{a}
